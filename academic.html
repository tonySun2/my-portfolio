Academic and Professional Background

I entered the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) as an Economics major after earning first place in the 2020 International Economics Olympiad China Final in high school. Early in my undergraduate studies, I realized that achieving my high school dream of becoming an economist required a stronger foundation in mathematics and statistics. This realization led me to transition to a double major in Mathematics/Economics and Statistics & Data Science, both of which have significantly broadened my analytical perspective.

While I initially enjoyed the theoretical aspects of economics, I became increasingly drawn to finance, where outcomes can be objectively measured by profits and losses. Over time, my interests evolved toward quantitative finance, a discipline that merges financial analysis with advanced mathematical and statistical techniques. It is a highly competitive field, attracting some of the brightest minds, and I am motivated to contribute meaningfully to it.

To meet the challenges of this rigorous field, I continuously refine my technical skills and expand my knowledge base through coursework and projects.
