Hi, my name is 孙文宏 (romanized: Wenhong Sun).

In English-speaking environments, I often go by Tony Sun, a name I like and find easy to connect with others.

Academic and Professional Background

I entered the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) as an Economics major after earning first place in the 2020 International Economics Olympiad China Final in high school. Early in my undergraduate studies, I realized that achieving my high school dream of becoming an economist required a stronger foundation in mathematics and statistics. This led me to switch to a double major in Mathematics/Economics and Statistics & Data Science, fields that have significantly broadened my analytical perspective.

While I initially enjoyed the theoretical aspects of economics, I found myself drawn to finance, where outcomes can be objectively measured by profits and losses. Over time, my interests shifted toward quantitative finance, a field that merges financial analysis with advanced mathematical and statistical techniques. It’s an extremely competitive discipline, attracting some of the brightest minds, and I’m motivated to contribute meaningfully to it. To meet the challenges of this rigorous field, I continuously refine my technical skills and expand my knowledge base with these courses and projects.

Personal Background

I grew up in Chengdu, China, a city famous for its rich history. The city itself dates back to 367 BC, and my high school recently celebrated its 2,160th anniversary, having been founded in 141 BC. Chengdu is also home to the world-famous Panda Research Center, where hundreds of pandas are cared for, and its hotpot is a must-try culinary experience.

Outside of academics, I’m a die-hard Chelsea Football Club fan—a passion that started in 2014 when I visited Stamford Bridge for the first time. While my childhood dream was to become a football commentator, I’ve long accepted that I’m better at watching football than playing it. However, I do have a knack for tennis, a sport I’ve been playing since I was nine. Since moving to the U.S. for college, I’ve also developed a love for hockey and now closely follow the NHL.

Beyond sports, I’m an avid movie enthusiast, particularly drawn to the works of Denis Villeneuve and Christopher Nolan. I also have a passion for photography—while I used to bring my Fujifilm XPro3 on every trip, I’ve recently embraced the convenience of phone photography, carrying my camera less often but still capturing moments wherever I go.
